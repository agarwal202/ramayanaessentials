The Ramayana written by Tulsidas known as Ramcharitmanas,is a sixteenth century epic. The story evolves around Rama,who was the seventh avatar of the Hindu God Vishnu. He is the central figure of the Hindu epic Ramayana,which is the principal narration of events connected to his incarnations on earth,his ideals and his greatness. His soul motive was to destroy evil forces on Earth and relieve kind people. He was the eldest son of King Dashratha-ruler of the Kosala Kingdom or Adhyodha, Present-day Uttar Pradesh. Rama's life and journey is one of adherence to dharma despite harsh treats and obstacles and many pairs of life and time. This adherence made him earn the name of Maryada Purushottam Ram which literally means The Perfect Man or Lord of Self-Control or Lord of Virtue. For the sake of his father's honour, Rama abandons his claim to Adhyodha's throne to serve an exile of fourtenn years in the forest. His wife Sita and brother Lakshmana decide to join him and all three spend the fourteen years in exile together. While in exile, Sita was kidnapped by Ravana,the demon king of Lanka, Present-day Sri Lanka. After a long and ardous search, Rama fights a colossal war against Ravana's armies. In a war of powerful and magical beings,greatly destructive weaponery and battles, Rama slays Ravana in battle and liberates his wife. Having completed his exile, Rama returns to be crowned king in Adhyodha and eventually becomes the emperor. The whole is is divided into seven parts known as Kandas.
